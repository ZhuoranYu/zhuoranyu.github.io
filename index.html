Zhuoran Yu

I am a Master student of Computer Science at Georgia Institute of Technology. I worked with Dr. Xu Chu on the topic of solving hard data management problems via machine learning techniques. Previously, I obtained my Bachelor of Mathematics, major in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics at the University of Waterloo. I worked as an undergraduate research assistant at Database System Group and my supervisor was Prof. Tamer Ozsu.

I'm currently working as a research intern at Sensetime, where I work on both providing algorithmic solutions to real-world products and optimizing current state-of-the-art object detectors, especially single-stage detectors.

Research

I'm generally interested application fields of deep learning. Currently, I'm working on fundamental problems of computer vision such as image processing and object detection. Project
Multi-View Sparse Human Detection in Kitchen

The main objective of this project is to build a detector which detects if dressings of chiefs match the specification in kitchens. My obligation is to provide object detection solutions to efficiently detect human heads and bodys captured by real-world cameras.
